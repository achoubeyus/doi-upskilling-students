## Get to Know : AI Foundry, Models, AI Services  

![Slide 8](slides_images/Slide8.PNG)

**Azure AI Foundry** is Microsoft’s unified AI platform on Azure, bringing together models, AI services, agent orchestration, safety, and observability under a single, governed boundary. It is designed to enable organizations to build, deploy, and operate AI applications and intelligent agents at enterprise scale.<br>

**Think of Azure AI Foundry** as the foundational AI layer on Azure, providing end‑to‑end capabilities across the AI application lifecycle:<br>
   - ***Model Access and Choice*** Azure Open AI model, third party model, flexabilities in selection of models<br> 
  
   - ***Azure AI Services***  Simplifies building multimodal and intelligent applications by composing services together.<br>
      - Azure AI Search, <br>
      - Vision<br>
      - Speech<br>
      - Language<br>
      - Document Intelligence<br>
      - Content Safety.<br>
   - ***Agent and Application Enablement***-  Includes built‑in capabilities for:<br>
      - Agent services and orchestration<br>
      - Retrieval‑Augmented Generation (RAG)<br>
      - Evaluation and experimentation<br>
      - Tracing, monitoring, and observability<br>
  - ***Enterprise Readiness and Governance***  - Designed for enterprise production workloads with:<br>
    - Enables responsible AI adoption with safety and governance embedded by design.

## What Is an AI Agent?

![Slide 11](slides_images/Slide11.PNG)


### Comments

Speaker notes: 
Let’s break down what we mean by an AI agent and how it differs from traditional automation or simple bots.
An AI agent is a micro-service that takes unstructured messages—like user questions or system events—optionally invokes other APIs or tools and returns meaningful responses.
Agents are powered by large language models (LLMs) and can follow instructions, use tools, access knowledge, perform actions, and retain memory of previous interactions.
The agent workflow typically involves three main steps: 
Input: The agent receives messages from users or systems.
Processing: The agent interprets the input, may call tools or APIs, and uses its memory and knowledge to reason and plan.
Output: The agent generates a response, which could be a message, an action, or a result from a tool.
Unlike rule-based bots, AI agents can handle more complex, iterative tasks, adapt to new information, and collaborate with other agents or humans.
Why This Matters: This architecture allows agents to solve a wide range of problems—from answering questions and retrieving data to executing multi-step workflows and integrating with external systems. Also, agents can be designed for specific roles, such as coding, analytics, customer support, or orchestration, and can work together in multi-agent systems for even more complex scenarios.
Next, we’ll look at the spectrum of LLM-based solutions, from simple one-shot tasks to sophisticated multi-agent systems.
